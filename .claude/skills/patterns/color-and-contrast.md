# Color and Contrast Patterns

## Role-based color assignment

Assign colors by role, not by preference:

- **Background:** neutral (white, off-white, dark, charcoal)
- **Primary text:** high contrast against background (near-black on light, near-white on dark)
- **Secondary text:** medium contrast (gray on light, light gray on dark)
- **Brand accent:** one color used sparingly for emphasis, CTAs, or highlights
- **CTA:** must contrast strongly against its immediate background

Never use the brand accent color as a background for body text.

## Contrast requirements

Minimum contrast ratios (WCAG):
- Normal text (< 18px): 4.5:1
- Large text (≥ 18px bold or ≥ 24px): 3:1
- UI components (buttons, inputs): 3:1 against adjacent color

For social posts: aim higher than minimum — feeds are viewed in bright light and at small sizes.

## Palette economy

- Limit unique hues to 2–3 per frame
- Use tints and shades of one hue rather than multiple unrelated colors
- One color should visually dominate (background)
- One color should create emphasis (accent)
- Text colors are neutral, not chromatic

## Dark mode logic

When converting to dark mode:
1. Background: near-black (not pure #000000 — use #0F0F0F to #1A1A1A)
2. Primary text: near-white (not pure #FFFFFF — use #F0F0F0 to #FAFAFA)
3. Brand accent: may need to be lightened or adjusted for contrast
4. Surfaces: use slight elevation through tonal shift (e.g., card = #1E1E1E on #141414 bg)
5. CTA button: ensure it still pops — may need to become lighter or use brand accent

## Color as information

Colors used for status, state, or meaning must be consistent:
- Do not reuse error red as a brand accent
- Do not reuse success green as a decorative element
- If color carries meaning, reinforce it with shape or text (don't rely on color alone)

## Muted/editorial palette

To achieve a refined, editorial feel:
- Desaturate accent colors by 20–40%
- Use warm off-whites (e.g., #F8F5F0) instead of pure white
- Use warm near-blacks (e.g., #1A1714) instead of pure black
- Reduce the number of chromatic elements — let typography carry the design
