# CTA Treatment Patterns

## Core principle

A CTA must be discoverable without being dominant.
It should be the most clickable thing, not the most visually loud thing.

## CTA placement

**Social posts:**
- Bottom third of the frame — after the user has absorbed the message
- Avoid top placement unless the design is deliberately CTA-first (unusual)
- Right-aligned or left-aligned — pick one and be consistent with the layout

**Web pages:**
- Primary CTA: above the fold in the hero
- Repeat at the end of each major section
- Final CTA: last section before the footer

**App screens:**
- Primary action: bottom safe zone (thumb-reachable)
- Minimum 44×44pt touch target

## CTA visual treatment

**Button:** Most common. Solid fill, rounded corners, or sharp corners matching brand style.
- Minimum height: 44px (web/app), 48px (social post)
- Minimum horizontal padding: 24px each side
- Label: 1–3 words, imperative verb first ("Shop now", "Get started", "Download free")

**Text link:** Use for secondary actions only. Never for primary CTA in promotional contexts.

**Ghost/outline button:** Use for secondary CTA alongside a primary solid button. Never as the only CTA.

## CTA hierarchy

In any single screen or frame, there should be:
- One primary CTA (highest visual weight)
- Zero or one secondary CTA (lower visual weight)
- Never two CTAs of equal weight competing

## CTA color logic

- Primary CTA: high contrast against its background (brand color or near-black on light bg, white or accent on dark bg)
- On white backgrounds: solid dark or brand-colored button
- On dark backgrounds: solid brand-color or white button
- Never: light button on light background, dark button on dark background

## CTA copy patterns

Strong: "Shop now", "Get started", "Download free", "Book a demo"
Weak: "Click here", "Submit", "Learn more" (vague), "Continue" (no incentive)

Match the CTA label to the conversion action precisely.
If the action is to download, say "Download." If it's to buy, say "Shop" or "Buy."

## CTA in variation context

When creating a color variation:
- Always verify the CTA still has sufficient contrast after color changes
- If the background changed to dark, the button may need to switch from dark to light

When creating a layout variation:
- The CTA must remain in a visually quiet zone
- Moving it to a busier area requires adding a container or overlay for contrast
