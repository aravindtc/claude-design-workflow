# Social Post Variation Types

A catalog of valid layout and color variation strategies for social posts.

## Layout variations

### Top-image / bottom-copy
Image fills the upper portion (50–65%), copy panel below.
Best for: product reveals, editorial fashion, clean promotional posts.
Works when: the image has a clear top-heavy subject or can be cropped to one.

### Full-bleed image with protected text panel
Image fills the entire frame, solid or semi-transparent panel protects text.
Best for: lifestyle, atmosphere-led content.
Works when: image has a clear zone (usually lower third) with less visual noise.

### Left-image / right-copy split
Vertical split — portrait image left, text column right.
Best for: fashion, product detail, feature announcements.
Works when: image has a portrait orientation or strong left-side subject.

### Right-image / left-copy split
Mirror of above.
Use when the image subject faces left or leads better from the right.

### Text-led with image as secondary proof
Typography is dominant, image is a smaller supporting element.
Best for: quote posts, announcements, editorial.
Works when: the message is stronger than the visual.

### Framed / contained image
Image sits inside a defined shape or bordered container, not full-bleed.
Best for: premium, editorial, structured brand communication.

### Centered stack
All elements centered vertically, reading top to bottom.
Best for: simple announcements, event posts, minimal offers.
Works when: the content is short enough not to crowd.

## Color variations

### Dark / premium mode
Replace light background with deep neutral (near-black, dark navy, charcoal).
Flip text to white or light. Accent color may need adjustment for contrast.
Best for: luxury, tech, premium fashion.

### Muted editorial palette
Desaturate accent colors, use warm neutrals and soft tones.
Reduce contrast slightly for a refined, magazine feel.
Best for: lifestyle, beauty, editorial brands.

### High-energy campaign palette
Amplify brand color saturation. Bold background. High contrast CTA.
Best for: sales events, launches, promotions.

### Monochrome with accent CTA
Single-color palette (blacks, whites, greys) with one strong accent only on the CTA.
Best for: premium minimalism, tech, finance.

### Brand-refined alternative
Keep the same structure and hierarchy. Replace one color in the palette with a refined alternative (e.g., navy instead of red, forest green instead of teal).
Best for: A/B testing brand color directions.

---

## Combination axes

When creating 2–3 variations, choose distinct axes:
- Layout A + Color A vs Layout B + Color B (two fully distinct directions)
- Same layout, different color strategy (pure color test)
- Same color, different layout structure (pure layout test)

Avoid: two layout variations with the same color. Avoid: two color variations with the same layout unless that is explicitly the brief.
