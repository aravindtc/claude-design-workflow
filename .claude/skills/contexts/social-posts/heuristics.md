# Social Post Heuristics

Visual heuristics specific to social media post design.

## Composition

- Use one dominant focal move — not many medium ones
- Give the headline enough room to breathe (minimum 40px clear space around it)
- Prefer one strong anchor zone for copy (top, center, or bottom — not scattered)
- Let the image carry mood and proof, not just fill space
- Accent shapes should support eye movement, not decorate
- Ensure the design reads clearly at thumbnail size (~300px)

## Hierarchy

- The eye should land on the most important element within 1 second
- Headline > supporting message > CTA is the standard flow for promotional posts
- CTA must be visually distinct but not compete with headline for dominance
- Avoid three elements of equal visual weight — one must lead

## Spacing

- Minimum 48px safe padding from frame edge for all text
- Consistent internal spacing: pick one gap unit and multiply (e.g., 16px, 32px, 48px)
- Do not push text to the edges — it reads as amateur and clips in some feed crops
- Large whitespace zones are intentional breathing — not empty accidents

## Text on image

- Never place text directly on visually noisy imagery without a contrast layer
- Use solid overlays, gradient overlays, blurred panels, or image repositioning
- Dark overlay opacity range: 0.65–0.85 for sufficient legibility
- Minimum contrast ratio for body text: 4.5:1 (WCAG AA)

## CTA treatment

- CTA button should sit in a visually quiet zone
- Minimum CTA size: 44px height for tap target
- CTA label should be short: 1–3 words
- Do not let the CTA disappear visually (contrast, size, placement all matter)

## Image strategy

- Portrait images work best in 1080×1350 (4:5 ratio)
- Square crops should have a clear subject centered or rule-of-thirds placed
- Full-bleed images need a protected text zone — plan the crop around it
- Do not squash or stretch images — use resize and crop, not distort

## Color

- Limit accent colors to 1–2 per frame
- Brand color should appear in at most 2–3 elements
- High-contrast accent on CTA is more effective than decorative use of brand color
- Dark mode variants need elevated contrast on all text (not just headline)
