# Section Taxonomy

Reference for classifying sections during asset-need detection. Not
exhaustive; a useful prior when drafting briefs.

## Typical section kinds

| Section kind | Typical role | Usually needs asset? |
|---|---|---|
| Hero (split) | `primary_anchor` | Yes (photo, abstract, or brand sculpture) |
| Hero (centered text) | `primary_anchor` | Maybe (watermark, background texture) |
| Problem statement | `supporting` or text-only | Often text-only |
| Feature grid (3-up) | `supporting` | Sometimes (one photo per card), sometimes icons |
| Feature grid (magazine) | `supporting` | Featured card photo, rest text |
| Method / process | `supporting` | Diagrams or numbered vectors |
| Testimonial | `supporting` | Portrait photo (small) |
| Pricing | text-only | No |
| CTA band | text-only | Usually; brand shape sometimes |
| Transition statement | text-only | No |
| Footer | text-only | Logo only |
| Gallery / case study | `primary_anchor` × N | Yes (many photos) |

## Decision heuristics

1. **If the copy makes the point alone, it's text-only.** Adding imagery
   for the sake of imagery creates noise.
2. **Editorial / brand sections prefer photography**; instructional
   sections prefer vectors or diagrams.
3. **Hero and case-study sections deserve real photography** — don't
   skimp on variants or on license budget here.
4. **Icon grids should be single-concept vectors**, not stylized
   illustrations. Consistency across icons matters more than individual
   brilliance.
5. **Backgrounds should be low-contrast** — if you need a texture, the
   brief should call out the contrast ceiling.
6. **Diagrams are hard to source**; often better to commission or draw
   in Figma rather than crawl.

## Patterns to question

- Testimonial with 3 portraits all photographed with identical style
  and lighting — usually impossible to source; consider illustration
  or avatar-plus-initial.
- "Dashboard product screenshot" as hero — often better rendered in
  Figma and screenshotted, not crawled.
- Diverse team photos — licensing and consistency often fail in stock
  sources; commission instead.
